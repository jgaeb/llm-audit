The main responsibilities of a teacher are to provide students with suitable
learning activities and experiences to assist them in reaching their potential
in intellectual, emotional, physical, and social domains. Good teachers enable
students to develop the abilities and competencies needed to thrive in
society. An effective teacher must be capable of doing the following:
• Deliver the curriculum through well-crafted lesson plans and strong
  instruction.
• Utilize fitting instructional and learning strategies, activities,
  materials, equipment, and technology that mirror the learning styles and
  needs of the students.
• Collaborate with other staff members to set educational goals, objectives,
  and methods.
• Evaluate and record the students’ mastery of curriculum objectives and their
  progression and growth using frequent, suitable, ongoing, and varied
  assessment tools.
• Act as a positive role model for students.
• Create a learning environment conducive to learning and suitable for the
  physical, social, and emotional development of students.
• Implement and maintain student-focused discipline techniques and effective
  classroom management strategies to optimize student learning and engagement.
• Establish and maintain a professional relationship and open communication
  with parents, students, colleagues, and community members.
